Exploring the Red Planet

Mars, humanity's next frontier in space exploration. Since the 1960s, dozens of missions have been sent to Mars, revealing the secrets of this red world. Explore the landers on Mars' surface, the orbiters circling above, and the space agencies driving these discoveries.

Mars Exploration Missions

Mars Landers

From early missions like Mars 3 to modern rovers like Curiosity and Zhurong, landers enable direct study of the Martian surface. These engineering marvels help us understand Mars' geology, climate, and potential habitability.

Mars Orbiters

Orbiting satellites provide a global perspective, helping map Mars, study its atmosphere, and serve as communication relays. From NASA's MRO to China's Tianwen-1 orbiter, these missions have transformed our understanding of Mars.

Discover the space agencies behind Mars missions. From NASA and ESA to CNSA and ISRO, learn how these organizations collaborate and compete to advance humanity's exploration of the Red Planet.
