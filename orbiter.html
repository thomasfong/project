Exploring Mars Orbit

Since the 1960s, humans have launched dozens of orbiters to Mars. These engineering marvels have expanded our understanding of the Red Planet, revealing its geology, atmosphere, and potential habitability secrets.

Mars Orbital Satellites

Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (MRO)

Launched by NASA in 2005, it carries the most powerful camera ever sent to Mars, with a resolution of 0.3 meters per pixel.

USA 2005-Present

Mars Express

ESA's first Mars mission, launched in 2003, discovered evidence of subsurface water and methane on Mars.

Europe 2003-Present

Mars Odyssey

NASA's long-running Mars orbiter, launched in 2001, has operated for over 20 years and discovered subsurface ice deposits.

USA 2001-Present

Mangalyaan

ISRO's first Mars mission, launched in 2013, successfully entered Mars orbit at an extremely low cost.

India 2013-Present

Tianwen-1 Orbiter

China's first Mars mission, successfully entered Mars orbit in 2021, carrying a high-resolution camera and mineral spectrometer.

China 2020-Present

Hope Probe

UAE's first interplanetary mission, entered Mars orbit in 2021, studying Mars' atmosphere and weather systems.

UAE 2020-Present

Mars Global Surveyor

Launched by NASA in 1996, operated until 2006, mapping the first global high-resolution map of Mars' surface.

USA 1996-2006

Mars Atmosphere and Volatile Evolution (MAVEN)

Launched by NASA in 2013, studying how Mars' atmosphere has been lost to space over time.

USA 2013-Present

Trace Gas Orbiter (TGO)

ESA-Roscosmos collaboration, launched in 2016, studying trace gases in Mars' atmosphere.

Europe/Russia 2016-Present
