Exploring Mars Surface

Since the 1990s, humans have sent robotic rovers to traverse Mars. These mobile laboratories have explored the Martian terrain, conducted experiments, and revealed secrets about the Red Planet's geology, climate, and potential for life.

Mars Rovers

Sojourner

NASA's first Mars rover (1997). Part of the Pathfinder mission, this microwave-sized rover proved rovers could operate on Mars, traveling 100 meters in 83 days.

USA 1997

Spirit

NASA's Mars Exploration Rover (2004-2010). Designed for 90-day mission but operated for 6 years, traveling 7.7 km and finding evidence of past water activity.

USA 2004-2010

Opportunity

Spirit's twin rover (2004-2018). Set distance record (45 km) and operated 60x longer than planned. Discovered clay minerals formed in water.

USA 2004-2018

Curiosity

Car-sized NASA rover (2012-present). Exploring Gale Crater, it discovered ancient freshwater environments and organic molecules essential for life.

USA 2012-Present

Perseverance

NASA's most advanced rover (2021-present). Searching for ancient microbial life, collecting samples for future return to Earth, and testing oxygen production.

USA 2021-Present

Zhurong

China's first Mars rover (2021-2022). Part of Tianwen-1 mission, explored Utopia Planitia, studying surface geology and subsurface structure.

China 2021-2022

Rosalind Franklin

ESA's upcoming ExoMars rover. Will drill 2 meters below surface to search for preserved organic material and signs of past life.

Europe Planned

Sample Fetch Rover

Future ESA rover (planned 2028). Will collect sample tubes left by Perseverance for return to Earth in Mars Sample Return campaign.
